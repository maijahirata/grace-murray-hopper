Grace Murray Hopper

Birthdate:

December 9th, 1906 in New York, NY.

Date of Death:

January 1st, 1992 at 85 years old, in Arlington, VA.

Contributions to Computer Science:

Grace Murray Hopper earned a master’s degree and a Ph.D. in mathematics from Yale and was most known for her astounding contributions to computer programming, software development and the design and implementation of programming languages. In 1949, Hopper joined the Eckert-Mauchly Computer Corporation in Philadelphia as a senior mathematician where she pioneered the idea of automatic programming and new ways to use a computer to code. In 1952, she developed the first compiler named A-0, which translated mathematical code into machine-readable code, which is the core of modern programming languages. In 1953, she proposed the idea of writing programs in words rather than symbols, and despite being told that her idea would not work, in 1956, she and her team were running FLOW-MATIC, or the first programming language to use word commands where she also demonstrated how programs could be written in word-based languages other than English.
